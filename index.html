Key evidence to share
Corticosteroid injection
Short-term benefit only (≤8 weeks). UK GRASP trial: no long-term benefit over 12 months. Useful only as a bridge to enable rehab, not a substitute for it.
Imaging
Ultrasound preferred over MRI for cost-effectiveness and comparable accuracy. Only needed if no improvement after 12 weeks of conservative care — unless partial tear suspected.
Active rehab evidence
2025 international Clinical Practice Guideline: active progressive rehab is gold standard. 2024 systematic review: scapular stabilisation significantly improves subacromial impingement outcomes.
